Through the review and implementation process, I gained valuable insights into several key areas, including HTML structure and semantic markup, as well as CSS organization and styling best practices. This experience significantly strengthened my overall understanding of development. The three most important concepts I learned are:

   I developed a stronger understanding of creating responsive layouts that adapt seamlessly across various screen sizes.    Ethical issues or risks associated with the use of AI in development

4. Loss of Context and Essential Knowledge:
   Overreliance on AI in development can lead to the erosion of important context and domain expertise, posing both ethical and practical challenges. This not only impacts code quality and future maintainability but also undermines developers’ own understanding and skills. When teams repeatedly use AI-generated solutions without fully grasping the underlying concepts, they may become disconnected from crucial aspects of the project. Sometimes, although AI-produced code functions correctly, it can overlook specific project requirements. This situation is especially challenging for newcomers like me. From an ethical standpoint, developers have a responsibility to thoroughly understand and be knowledgeable about the systems they create.

5. Loss of Core Skills, Unquestioning Trust, and Lack of Critical Evaluation:

Relying on AI for tasks like problem-solving or debugging can push developers to lose technical skills and deep understanding of the issue. Accepting AI-generated code without concept understanding and careful review increases the risk of unnoticed bugs, and logic errors. This reduces expertise critical thinking, accountability, and can lead to technical debt over time. AI is useful for learning concepts, but I should limit its use to avoid undermining my critical thinking.

3.  Security and Privacy risks:
    I use AI tools for quick assistance because they provide fast and seemingly accurate results. Occasionally, I intentionally paste my code for debugging without considering the long-term consequences. This habit can have significant effects once I become a developer, as the code may contain organizational information or include copyrighted or insecure patterns, raising the risk of vulnerabilities. Therefore, I'm planning to train myself to use AI strictly for reference purposes only.
